Many people make the mistake of thinking that heartburn is only caused by too much stomach acid. Most heartburn sufferers are not producing too much stomach acid. Instead their symptoms are more likely caused by a little acid in the wrong place. The stomach is the only part of the digestive system which is actually supposed to contain acid - it is there to help break food down. The pain associated with heartburn is felt when small amounts of acid from the stomach flow back into the gullet or food pipe causing the familiar burning sensation.
