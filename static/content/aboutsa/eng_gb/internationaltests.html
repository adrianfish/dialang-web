DIALANG and other international language tests

In some languages learners can take international language tests, that is, tests that are administered and recognised in many countries. As with some school-based or national tests, some of these international tests can give you a fairly good idea where you stand against an international yardstick such as the Council of Europe six-point scale. However, you need to study what the test producers say about the meaning of their grades: some may report their results by using descriptions which are different from those used in the Council of Europe scale. To what extent do the two descriptions match or differ?

Note that some international tests are specific to certain occupations or fields, which limits their comparability with DIALANG.
