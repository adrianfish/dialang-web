You use only some of the language skills

Language proficiency is complex. It consists of a number of skills or areas that you can master at different levels of proficiency. It may be that you mainly read in the language that you are learning, or that you use it mainly for oral communication, and you may have little experience using some of the skills. Your problems - or lack of problems - when using a particular skill may affect your perception of how good you are at using the other skills.

For example, you may overestimate your skills because you are fairly good in one particular skill. Or you may underestimate your skills because you encounter problems with a particular skill.
